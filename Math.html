Congruent

Has equal size and shape.

Concurrent

Three or more lines that intersect at one point.

Concave

A polygon whose diagonal lines can only be inside.

Convex

A polygon whose diagonal lines can only be outside.

Consecutive lines

Two sides with a common endpoint.

Arc

A portion of a circle that can be half of a circle, the majority of a circle, or a minor section of a circle.

Inscribed Angle

An angle whose vertex is on the circle with two connecting chords.

Intercepted Arc

The arc that is inside the inscribed angle and whose endpoints are on the angle.

Theorem

A claim that can be proved to be true using well-accepted mathematical procedures and justifications.

Radii

A plural for radius.

Equidistant

Each object in the set is equally distanced from that point.

Tangent

A straight line that meets a curve or curved surface at a particular place but does not cross it when extended.

Coplanar

Set of points or lines on the same plane.

Segment Bisector

A line or a segment that cuts a line segment in half.

Skew lines

Skewed lines are two lines that are not parallel to one another, nor do they intersect.
