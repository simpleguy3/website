Abundant

existing or available in large quantities; plentiful.

Acute

Bad, difficult, or unwelcome situation or phenomenon, present or experienced to a severe or intense degree.

Ammonification

When an organism excretes waste or dies, the nitrogen in its tissues is in the form of organic nitrogen.

Assimilation

The absorption and digestion of food or nutrients by the body or any biological system.

Capillarity

The tendency of a liquid in a capillary tube or absorbent material to rise or fall as a result of surface tension.

Chronic

Persisting for a long time or constantly recurring.

Combustion

The process of burning something.

Condenses

Change or cause to change from a gas or vapor to a liquid.

Erosion

The geological process in which earthen materials are worn away and transported by natural forces such as wind or water.

Hospitable

Pleasant and favorable for living in.

Nitrification

Nitrification is the biological oxidation of ammonia to nitrite followed by the oxidation of the nitrite to nitrate occurring through separate organisms or direct ammonia oxidation to nitrate in comammox bacteria.

Percolation

The movement of water through the soil itself.

Precipitation

Water that falls from the atmosphere to the Earth's surface.

Stunting

Impaired growth and development that children experience from poor nutrition, repeated infection, and inadequate psychosocial stimulation.

Wasting

Causing a person or a part of the body to become progressively weaker and more emaciated.
